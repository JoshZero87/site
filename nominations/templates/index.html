Our Nomination Process

Our Revolution seeks to empower the next generation of progressive leaders by inspiring and supporting progressive candidates to run for offices across the entire spectrum of government. From school boards to congressional seats, a new generation of political leaders, dedicated to transforming America’s corrupt campaign finance system and rigged economy, will be elected into office.

In order to transform our country, we need everyone - progressive activists, women of color, immigrants, Native Americans, religious minorities, working class americans, and members of marginalized communities - to be elected into office at all levels.

We value the power of local grassroots organizing and because of that, only candidates or initiatives that have been nominated by local Our Revolution groups, or strategically identified by OR staff and supported by the local group, will be considered for an Our Revolution national endorsement.

  1. Complete a nomination form for your candidate or initiative
  2. If a candidate endorsement, have your candidate fill out our candidate questionnaire
  3. Submit the nomination

Our Revolution will provide leaders inspired by the political revolution with the following resources on a race-by-race basis.

  • Grassroots Fundraising
  • GOTV Texting
  • Phonebanking
  • Grassroots Support
  • Promotion Through Web and Social

To be considered for an endorsement, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Receive a nomination from an Our Revolution group, including its members. Please visit our local groups map to locate an Our Revolution group near you.
  • If there is no local group in your race location, email your complete candidate questionnaire to political@ourrevolution.com. Members of Our Revolution in the area will be surveyed to assess the level of local support for the race.
  • Be willing to run a grassroots powered campaign.
  • Agree with the Our Revolution platform.
  • Run a positive campaign.
  • Reject money from corporate interests.

Note: Nominations submitted less than 30 days before the election are unlikely to be considered.
